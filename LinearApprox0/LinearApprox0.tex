\begin{question} \label{Q9e8f3rg}
The function
\[
   T = f(x,y)
\]
exrpresses the temperature (in Celsius degrees) of a point in the $xy$-plane in terms of its coordinates (in meters).

Suppose
\begin{enumerate}
\item $f(5,4) = 12$ ,

\item 
\[
      \frac{\partial T}{\partial x}\Big|_{(x,y) = (5,4)} = -2, 
\]
and
\item 
\[
      \frac{\partial T}{\partial y}\Big|_{(x,y) = (5,4)} = 3, 
\]

\end{enumerate}

Now do the following.

\begin{enumerate}
\item Approximate the temperature at the point $(5.2, 4,3)$.

\item Find a linear function $L(x,y)$ that approximates the temperature function at the point with coordinates $(x,y)$. Assume $(x,y) \sim (5,4)$.

\item What additional information might you need to know about the function $f$ to put a bound on the error in your approximation?

\end{enumerate}


\end{question}
